Problem → Solution → Findings
🔴▶ PROBLEM
  • Static reorder points were set once during ERP implementation and never updated — 43% of SKUs had reorder points based on demand data more than 18 months old, leading to systematic under-ordering in growing lines
  • Safety stock calculations used a flat buffer of "2 weeks demand" across all products regardless of demand volatility, lead time variance, or service level requirements — over-stocking slow movers and under-buffering volatile fast movers simultaneously
  • Planners received no automated alert when stock fell below reorder point — the first indication was a stockout notification from the warehouse, by which point it was already too late to avoid a service failure
  • Suggested order quantities were calculated manually in Excel without considering economic order quantities, moq constraints, or lead time coverage — resulting in either under-ordering (requiring emergency top-ups) or over-ordering (inflating working capital)
🔵▶ SOLUTION
  • Dynamic ROP formula: ROP = (AvgDemand × AvgLT) + Z × √(LT × σD² + D² × σLT²) — recalculates automatically when either demand or lead time data is refreshed from the ERP
  • Service level Z-scores applied per SKU tier: A-class products at 99% (Z=2.33), B-class at 97.5% (Z=1.96), C-class at 95% (Z=1.65) — aligning buffer investment with product revenue contribution
  • Colour-coded daily reorder alert list generated at 07:00 each morning: red = PO required today, amber = PO required within 5 days, green = stock adequate — pushed to buyers via email and Teams notification
  • Suggested order quantity includes minimum order quantity constraints, economic order quantity guidance, and lead time coverage calculation to prevent both under and over-ordering
🟢▶ FINDINGS
  • Dynamic ROP recalculation reduced stockout frequency by 38% in the first quarter — primarily by catching 14 SKUs where demand had grown 40%+ but static ROPs had not been updated
  • ABC-tiered service levels freed up €67,000 in working capital previously tied in excess safety stock on slow-moving C-class products that had been over-buffered at the same rate as A-class SKUs
  • Automated morning alert removed the need for planners to manually query the ERP each day — saving 1.5 hours per planner per day across a team of 4 buyers, equivalent to 3 FTE-weeks per month
  • Emergency purchase order frequency fell from 23/month to 6/month — reducing premium freight costs and supplier expediting fees by €18,400/month
  • Average days-between-stockout improved from 11 to 47 days across the monitored SKU portfolio

💡 Advice for Companies with Similar Challenges
Dynamic Reorder Point Implementation — Key Recommendations
  • Dynamic reorder points tied to rolling 13-week demand averages and current lead time actuals outperform static ROP settings by 35% on stockout frequency. Rebuild your reorder logic every quarter minimum — or automate the refresh entirely from your ERP data.
  • Never apply a single safety stock formula across your entire SKU portfolio. Tier your service levels by product value contribution: A-class at 99%, B-class at 97.5%, C-class at 95%. This frees up 15–25% of working capital in safety stock while actually improving service levels on your most important products.
  • The most dangerous phrase in inventory planning is "I'll check that later." Automate your reorder alerts and make them impossible to ignore. A daily push notification to buyers when a SKU breaches its ROP costs almost nothing to build but prevents the majority of stockout events before they occur.
  • Always validate your lead time assumptions against actual GR data, not contracted lead times. In most companies, actual lead time is 20–40% longer than what's in the system. Using contracted lead times for ROP calculations systematically under-calculates safety stock and causes avoidable stockouts.
// KEY TAKEAWAY

Static reorder points are a silent killer of service levels. They feel stable because nothing obviously breaks — until suddenly everything does at once. The shift from static to dynamic ROPs, refreshed from rolling demand and live lead time data, is one of the highest-ROI improvements available to any inventory planning team. It costs almost nothing to implement and delivers compounding service-level improvements every quarter.
